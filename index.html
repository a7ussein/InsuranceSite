Questions, answered

I'm not in New Hampshire. Can you still be my advisor? +
Yes. The practice is licensed in all 50 states, so wherever you — or someone you'd refer — happen to be, the same advisory relationship applies. And if you move, your coverage and your advisor move with you.
How do you actually get paid? +
Honestly: by commission from the carrier you select, built into the same premium you'd pay going direct — you never pay the practice a fee. Because the appointment isn't tied to any single carrier, there is no incentive to place you in the wrong product. Retained clients matter more here than any single sale.
How is this different from a big-brand agent? +
A captive agent represents one company's products. This practice is independent — recommendations are drawn from more than twenty carriers, and they change whenever the market does, including at your renewal.
I'm already insured. Is a review worth it? +
Usually. Rates and replacement costs move every year, and most policies haven't been re-shopped in three or more. A review takes thirty minutes; the worst outcome is written confirmation that your current program is sound.
Should I call you before filing a claim? +
Yes — always. Whether a claim is worth filing depends on its type, your carrier, and your history. Part of the advisory role is telling you honestly when filing serves you and when it doesn't.
How quickly can coverage be in place? +
Life insurance timelines vary based on the carrier and underwriting requirements, but you'll have a straight, transparent timeline before we begin.
